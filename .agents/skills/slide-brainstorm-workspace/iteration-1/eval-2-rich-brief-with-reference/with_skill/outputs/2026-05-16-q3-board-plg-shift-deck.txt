─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  NARRATIVE ARC
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Core thesis:
    Outbound got us from zero to here.
    The market is now buying differently.
    The leverage moves from reps to the product.

  Story spine — one question per slide:

    01  TITLE              —  Why are we here?
    02  WHERE WE STAND     —  What did outbound earn us?
    03  WHAT'S BREAKING    —  Why won't more reps work?
    04  THE SHIFT          —  What changes, in one sentence?
    05  IN PRACTICE        —  What does it look like on a Tuesday?
    06  THE PLAN           —  What are we actually doing?
    07  THE ASK            —  What do we need from this room?

  Run order:
    Linear. No demo. No deep-dive appendix in the deck itself —
    detail decks (unit economics, hiring plan, funnel instrumentation)
    live in the data room and are referenced verbally if asked.


─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  DESIGN PRINCIPLES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Say less, mean more.
    • One idea per slide. Nothing else.
    • Sentences are short. Periods are loud.
    • One italic purple accent word per H2. Never more.
    • Word budget per slide: 25–40 words. Title < 15.

  Hairlines, not boxes.
    • Card grids look spreadsheety. We avoid them.
    • Use vertical and horizontal hairline rules to divide space.
    • Let whitespace do the column work.
    • Allow ONE soft purple wash per deck — used as a focal point, not a frame.
    • Every slide looks visually distinct from its neighbours.

  Card budget for the entire deck: 1.   (the slide 07 ask-wash)

  Board-tuned voice.
    • Operator language, not founder hype. "Worked." not "Crushed it."
    • Honor what got us here before pivoting away from it.
    • The numbers are placeholders [bracketed] — to be filled in
      with verified figures during build.

  NOTES ON THIS DRAFT  (initial draft · no previous version)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Why this shape (7 slides)
    The brief asked for 7 slides. With a board audience, that's the right
    count — enough to seat the pivot in evidence (02–03), name it (04),
    show it (05), commit to it (06), and ask for support (07). Anything
    longer drifts into appendix territory; anything shorter skips the
    "what's breaking" diagnosis that earns the pivot.

  Why a single soft wash on slide 07
    The Apple/Cursor reference deck spends its one card on the climax.
    For a board, the climax is the ASK, not the thesis — operators and
    investors know the thesis the moment they read the title. They've
    come to vote on what we want from them. Slide 07 earns the wash.

  Why slide 04 is the pivot, not the opener
    Restraint principle: "the narrative arc earns the thesis." Opening
    with "we're going PLG" would feel like a memo; landing it at the
    geometric centre of the deck — after the wins are honored (02)
    and the friction is named (03) — makes the pivot feel inevitable.

  Why placeholders for numbers
    Real ARR / CAC / cycle figures need to come from the data room
    and be cross-checked with the CFO before this deck is built.
    All numbers are marked [X] / [Y] / [Z] / [W] with a "NUMBERS TO
    FILL IN" subsection on slides 02 and 03 listing exactly what
    goes where.
