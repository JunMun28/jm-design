─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  NARRATIVE ARC
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Core thesis:
    AI isn't a replacement for what you do.
    It's a faster first draft of the parts you already redo every week.
    You bring the wisdom. It buys you back an evening.

  Story spine — one question per slide:

    01  TITLE              —  Why are we sitting here tonight?
    02  WHAT THIS ISN'T    —  Can I trust this in a classroom?
    03  WHERE IT HELPS     —  Which parts of prep, exactly?
    04  A TUESDAY EVENING  —  What does this look like for one real lesson?
    05  IN THEIR WORDS     —  What do other teachers actually type?
    06  TRY ONE THIS WEEK  —  What's the smallest thing I can try?


─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  DESIGN PRINCIPLES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Sound like a colleague, not a vendor.
    • First-person plural is welcome. "Let's name the worries."
    • Sentences can carry a comma when it sounds human. Don't force two-word
      stadium chants — that's the cold-tech voice this deck is rejecting.
    • Periods are still loud, just on slightly longer sentences.
    • One italic sage accent word per H2. Pick the word that flips meaning.

  Earn trust before asking for it.
    • Slide 02 names the worries out loud before pitching anything.
    • Every claim is small, concrete, and falsifiable. No "AI will transform
      education." Yes "Rewrite this passage at a 3rd-grade reading level."
    • The presenter is a peer — instructional coach, fellow teacher — not a
      district vendor. Footer is a name, not a logo block.

  Warm restraint, not cold restraint.
    • Parchment background. Warm ink. Sage accent. No pure black, no pure
      white, no neon. Hairlines are faded, not crisp.
    • Cards are last resort. Card budget for the entire deck: 1 (the soft
      sage wash on slide 06, used as a focal point).
    • No mono uppercase kickers — those read "tech product." Use a small
      italic serif label instead.
    • No tech-grid progress bar at the top. A quiet "01 / 06" page indicator
      in the top right margin, like a book corner.

  Adjacent slides never share a layout.
    • Variety map at the bottom of this file proves it.

  This is the initial draft. No previous version exists.

  Standing assumptions (made because interactive intake was skipped):

  Audience
    · K-12 mixed-subject teachers at a public school in-service / PD slot.
    · Mostly non-technical. Skeptical-but-curious about AI. Pressed for
      time. Care about their students more than about tools.

  Goal
    · Training / awareness, not adoption mandate. The deck asks for ONE
      small experiment, not a workflow change.

  Scope
    · Lesson planning specifically — drafting, adapting, differentiating,
      translating, parent comms. NOT grading. NOT student-facing chatbots.
      NOT classroom management. These boundaries are stated explicitly on
