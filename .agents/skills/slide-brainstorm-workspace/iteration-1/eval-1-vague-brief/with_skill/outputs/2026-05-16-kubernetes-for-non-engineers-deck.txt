─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  NARRATIVE ARC
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Core thesis:
    Kubernetes isn't a product. It's a place where software lives.
    It runs the runway. You set the route.
    Non-engineers don't operate it. They do budget for it, plan around
    it, and ask better questions because of it.

  Story spine — one question per slide:

    01  TITLE          —  Why are we here?
    02  THE METAPHOR   —  What even is Kubernetes? (in one picture)
    03  THE SHIFT      —  Why did we need it?
    04  WHAT IT DOES   —  Three jobs it does, all the time.
    05  IN PRACTICE    —  Show me where it shows up in our product.
    06  THE TRADE      —  What does it cost us?
    07  WHEN IT BREAKS —  How does an outage look from where I sit?
    08  ON MONDAY      —  What do I do with this on Monday?

  Run order:
    Linear · no demo · ~90 seconds per slide. Slides 02–04 carry the
    teaching weight; 05–07 ground it in their reality; 08 hands them
    the one sentence they're allowed to forget everything else for.


─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  DESIGN PRINCIPLES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Say less, mean more.
    • One idea per slide. Nothing else.
    • Sentences are short. Periods are loud.
    • One italic purple accent word per H2. Never more.
    • Word budget per slide: 25–40 words. Title < 15.

  Hairlines, not boxes.
    • Card grids look spreadsheety. We avoid them.
    • Vertical and horizontal hairline rules divide space.
    • Whitespace does the column work.
    • ONE soft purple wash for the entire deck — the final takeaway.
    • Every slide must feel visually distinct from its neighbours.

  No jargon without a metaphor.
    • If the audience would Google a word, we don't use it.
    • "Container" → "small program". "Node" → "computer".
    • "Pod" never appears. "kubectl" never appears.

  Card budget for the entire deck: 1.   (used on slide 07)

  Voice

    – One italic accent word per H2. The word that, if removed, would

    – Metaphors do the teaching; jargon stays out.
    – "kubectl", "YAML", "container", "pod", "node" do not appear in
