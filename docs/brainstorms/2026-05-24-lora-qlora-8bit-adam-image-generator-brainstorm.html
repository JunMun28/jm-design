LoRA, QLoRA, and 8-bit Adam for Custom Image Models

Core idea

Custom style does not require retraining the whole model.

Proposal: Teach LoRA, QLoRA, and 8-bit Adam through a Mosaic-inspired internal brand image generator.

Why it matters: The team learns both the mechanism and the practical path from examples to a usable custom model.

What makes it credible: Grab's Mosaic example shows an internal image generator aligned to visual identity; the training mechanics are grounded in LoRA, QLoRA, and bitsandbytes sources.

Slide 05

Training is a pipeline, not a magic upload

Examples become captions. Captions train adapters. Review makes it usable.

01 Collect brand examples

Illustrations, product scenes, approved campaign images.

02 Caption style rules

Subject, composition, tone, exclusions, brand motifs.

03 Train LoRA adapter

Base image model frozen; adapter learns style behavior.

04 Test prompt set

Repeatable prompts reveal consistency and failure modes.

05 Human review loop

Approve, reject, tag issues, iterate the adapter.

ASSUMPTION: This is the proposed internal workflow for a Mosaic-inspired brand image generator.
