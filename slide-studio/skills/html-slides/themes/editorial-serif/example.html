The case for slow softwareThe Margin · Essay № 14
The premise

Speed without judgement is just rework with a better name

Every team I have watched chase velocity eventually pays it back with interest. The feature ships on Friday and the incident lands on Monday.

Slow software is not the absence of speed. It is the discipline of doing the thinking once, in public, before code makes that thinking permanent.

In the margin

"Fast" usually means "deferred." The cost does not disappear; it moves downstream to whoever is on call.

Deliberate teams front-load the cost where it is cheapest: the conversation.

The discipline

Three habits separate deliberate teams from merely busy ones

01

Decide in prose before code

A one-page memo is cheaper to argue with than a merged pull request.

02

Ship the smallest honest version

Not the smallest demo — the smallest thing you would put your name on and maintain.

03

Leave the campsite legible

The next reader is the real user. Name things plainly and delete bravely.

The case for slow softwareThe discipline
Three habits, in order of leverage: decide in prose, ship the smallest honest version, leave the code legible. Spend most time on the first — it is where deliberate teams actually differ. Read titles, not full paragraphs.
